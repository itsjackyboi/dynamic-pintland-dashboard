Aleforge — The Brewing Heart of Pintland
Six months have passed since Drake's banishment, the Dread Harrow's assault, and the fall of the MAA. Aleforge has a mayor — Seamus Bonehardy, elected shortly after the Stewards' events. He has done absolutely nothing with the office. The town runs on its own momentum, its ale-stained streets alive with rumour, low-grade anxiety, and the particular frustration of a people who voted and got silence in return.
Voter Blocs & Occupational Sectors
Brewers
Favorable
The Kings secured ale production through policy. They are seen as patrons of the craft.
Current Sentiment
Approval72%

The flat brewer's tax and the Gilded Tankard grant under Drake's era were continued. The Kings' anti-blight ritual saved John Cum's fields, sparing a catastrophic shortage. Six months on, barley supplies are stable and Rotto trade routes flow freely. Most brewers would toast to any King sitting in the mayor's chair — though Wepple's gambling debts quietly unsettle the guild leadership.

Key Concern

Wepple's loss of Goldcoral investment money has created tension with Jack Anqoak. Some fear the Kings will meddle in private brew business if one is elected mayor.

Farmers
Favorable
John Cum's death grieves them deeply. Farm subsidies & the ritual cleansing earn the Kings loyalty.
Current Sentiment
Approval65%

The Thatcher statue was beloved. The $2000 farm loan programme rebuilt confidence after the blight crisis. John Cum's passing — and his nationally planned funeral — has made the farming community feel genuinely seen. The Kings publicly mourned him. That matters in the fields.

Key Concern

Without Cum, the single-supplier problem is dangerously exposed. New farms are growing but slowly. Farmers fear any new mayor will forget them the moment the barrels are full again.

Traders & Merchants
Cautious
Free trade deals are welcome, but instability and MAMA threats unsettle commerce.
Current Sentiment
Approval48%

Gideon's Rotto Free Trade Deal and elimination of sea tolls benefited merchants enormously. But six months of caretaker rule under the Kings — who are warriors, not administrators — has merchants worried. Who enforces contracts now? Who arbitrates disputes? The Barley Road Initiative promises connectivity but costs real coin.

Key Concern

MAMA extremists have disrupted two market days with protest and rumour-spreading. Traders hate instability above all else. The books Rump cooked have also leaked into merchant gossip, eroding trust in financial record-keeping.

Mystics
Favorable
The anti-blight ritual legitimised mysticism. The Kings are seen as spiritually open-minded.
Current Sentiment
Approval70%

The Cloister beast's defeat — by the Kings' own hands — is viewed as a sacred act. The mystic community now uses the Cloister as a holy meeting place by the Kings' invitation. The ritual cleansing of John Cum's fields confirmed the world's spiritual dimensions are real, not superstition. Six months on, mystics hold unprecedented social standing.

Key Concern

Fentmaxxer's heretical legacy still shadows legitimate mysticism. Many commonfolk cannot distinguish a true mystic from a warden-worshipper. The community desperately wants formal legitimacy from whoever becomes mayor.

Pirates & Drifters
Mixed
They love the Kings personally, but resent the growing structure and road tolls.
Current Sentiment
Approval55%

The Kings represent pirate-blood legend. Buke's dockhand roots and JP's wild history resonate with Aleforge's rough edges. The Shanty Town Defense Pact was celebrated. But road tolls increased 50%, and this crowd pays them. The ghost of Sackbeard's Dread Harrow still flies above the town — some find it heroic, others find it an ill omen.

Key Concern

If a King becomes mayor, will they become too respectable? Pirates fear losing the chaos that makes Aleforge worth visiting. Some have already drifted to Shanty Town.

Craftsmen & Miners
Supportive
Military spending boosted orders. The Kings' rule has been good for trades.
Current Sentiment
Approval66%

The military spending bill brought contracts for armour and weapons. Road construction under the Barley Road Initiative is creating consistent paid work. The Thatcher statue commission kept stoneworkers employed. Six months on, craftsmen are busy — which means they are content.

Key Concern

The Veilwalker Artifact Tariff blocked some profitable trade in enchanted tools. Some craftsmen believe the 150% smuggling penalty is excessive and has made Fenwick goods impossible to work with legally.

MAMA (Underground)
Hostile
Emboldened by the Kings' caretaker status. Plinket's sentence has only fuelled their rage.
Current Sentiment
Opposition92%

After the Stewards' events, MAMA received a cryptic message signed "SP / JR" — believed to be Plinket under a pseudonym. The child who survived the MAA massacre now leads a wing of the group. They call the Maximilian killing a political assassination. Six months of watching the Kings avoid mayoral accountability has radicalised the moderates within MAMA.

Key Concern

A strike is being planned. Their message reads: "Our time is soon." They are waiting for the Kings to show vulnerability. A contested mayoral election could be their moment.

Unemployed & Poor
Uncertain
Farm loans helped somewhat. The absence of stable government leaves many without recourse.
Current Sentiment
Approval38%

The Kings are heroes to the tavern crowd, but heroes rarely fix leaky roofs. The absence of a permanent mayor means no clear poverty relief. The unemployed are vulnerable to MAMA recruitment. Several have already joined, drawn by the promise of purpose and bread. The "Beggar" figure in town is a known barometer — when he's fed and sober, things are fine.

Shanty Town — Cliffs of Broken Masts
Shanty Town watched the Dread Harrow sail through a portal to Hell — and return to float above Aleforge. To the Windsunk Council, this is a declaration: the past never truly rests. Six months on, old salts drink uneasily and whisper about Sackbeard's legacy.
Windsunk Council
Wary
Impressed by the Kings' bravery, but deeply uneasy that Aleforge now holds an eternal ghost ship.
Current Sentiment
Trust in Aleforge42%

The Defense Pact still stands. The ale flows. But the Council did not sign up for Sackbeard's ghost armada hovering over their trade route. Old Salty has argued in three council sessions that the Dread Harrow must be burned or removed. The Kings have ignored this. Six months of silence on the matter has made it an open wound.

The Council is also disturbed that Drake — one of their own pirate lineage — was banished rather than tried by pirate law. They had no seat at that table.

Tide Callers
Troubled
They believe the portal event has disturbed the ocean's divine order. Ill omens abound.
Current Sentiment
Faith in Aleforge Kings28%

To the Tide Callers, breaching the Nether is an affront to the ocean's sovereignty. They held ritual mourning when the portal opened. Six months on, they claim the seas near the factory are running cold and strange, fish acting wrong, currents shifting. They blame the Kings for not sealing the factory properly after the incident.

Pirate Captains & Crews
Admiring
The Kings fought Sackbeard's cursed crew hand to hand. That earns real respect.
Current Sentiment
Admiration74%

Closing a portal by flying aboard the Dread Harrow itself? That's the stuff of Books of Captains legend. Ben (Jagerbauhm) in particular — flying higher than any man before — is being sung about in Shanty taverns. Six months on, young pirates are mimicking his leap in cliff-jumping competitions. The Kings have become myth whilst still living.

Coral-Eyed
Deeply Hostile
The Nether portal reminds them of what took their humanity. They want it destroyed utterly.
Current Sentiment
Trust in Kings12%

Those cursed by long exposure to the trench feel the factory's Nether breach like an ache in their transformed bones. The Kings closed the portal but left the dimension accessible. They grow more aggressive in their demands for total destruction of the Factory. Two have been heard speaking in tongues near the Shanty cliffs — locals say they are "calling down the trench."

Ashen Oath
Fanatic
Six months has emboldened their purge ideology. They view the Harrow as proof Pintland is corrupted.
Current Sentiment
Radicalism88%

Hidden within Shanty Town, the Ashen Oath have never been louder in their private cells. The events at Aleforge — piracy, portals, mysticism, beast-worship — confirm their doctrine: the world is too stained by grog and chaos to be reformed. Only fire cleanses. They are in quiet communion with unknown parties. The Windsunk Council does not know how large this faction has grown.

Drifters & Traders
Opportunistic
The Dread Harrow's return has sparked a tourism trade. Strangers come to see the ghost ship.
Current Sentiment
Economic Optimism67%

Word has spread across the seas: a ship from Hell floats above a brewing town. Curious merchants, scholars, and thrill-seekers are arriving. Shanty drifters are charging passage on boats to view the Harrow from below. Rotto traders are capitalising on this too, which creates friction. But coin is coin.

Providence — The Tonic Citadel
Providence received a new pilgrim six months ago — Cheeks, the blackmailer turned devoted aspirant, finally arrived to join the Church of Patmos. He brought with him secrets of Aleforge corruption that have set Cardinal Addy's mind whirring. The wheat deal with Aleforge still holds, but the holy city watches its ally uneasily.
Cardinal Addy & the Hierarchy
Calculating
The Rump scandal, delivered by Cheeks, is a divine gift. Providence now holds leverage over Aleforge.
Current Sentiment
Strategic Interest in Aleforge78%

Cheeks arrived with detailed knowledge of John Rump's embezzlement and illicit tonic use. Cardinal Addy has filed this information carefully and said nothing publicly — yet. The upcoming mayoral election in Aleforge is too valuable a moment to spend leverage foolishly. The Cardinal is praying for guidance on the optimal moment to act.

Six months on, the Church has also elevated Cheeks rapidly through the ranks — his blackmail instincts are being reframed as "divine intelligence gathering."

Apostles
Unsettled
The tonic's supply chain depends on Rotto — who now trades factory artifacts. This is deeply concerning.
Current Sentiment
Stability of Faith61%

The Apostles run on ClockHeart Tonic. The Tonic requires red-earth mineral from Rotto. And Rotto is now reportedly handling artifacts from the factory — a hellish portal site. To the devout, this is contamination by proximity. Some Apostles have begun praying for an alternative mineral source. The Cardinal has not addressed this publicly, which is itself a source of anxiety.

Friars & Sextons
Anti-Aleforge
Drake's banishment to Vodkonia, not Providence, is seen as an insult — he should face a church trial.
Current Sentiment
Resentment of Kings73%

Drake handled stolen artifacts — items of dimensional power — and the Kings simply exiled him. No church consultation. No theological tribunal. Friars believe that a man with Veilwalker blood who trafficked in Nether-adjacent artifacts is precisely the kind of heretic Providence was built to judge. The Kings dismissed this without a second thought.

Califarts & Scholars
Intensely Curious
Drake's Veilwalker heritage and the Cloister link to New Haven has upended their study of the tonic.
Current Sentiment
Scholarly Excitement82%

The revelation that a Veilwalker — an earth-born — could curse the living with eternal life overlaps dangerously with Califart research into tonic immortality properties. Six months of debate in the towers have produced a heretical working theory: the ClockHeart Tonic may share an origin with Veilwalker biology. The Cardinal has not yet seen these notes.

Traders & Smiths
Content
The Providence Wheat Deal was a solid win. Brewing tool exports continue to rise.
Current Sentiment
Economic Satisfaction69%

While the theological class frets about Nether portals and Veilwalker blood, Providence's merchant layer just wants to sell brewing tools. Ale production being stabilised in Aleforge means more demand for Providence-built stills and fermentation equipment. Six months of uninterrupted trade has padded the pockets nicely. They wish the church would talk less about judgment and more about contracts.

Recent Converts (incl. Cheeks)
Zealous
Cheeks is already legendary in the novitiates. His "intelligence of faith" is considered a calling.
Current Sentiment
Devotional Zeal91%

Cheeks arrived with his Rump payments in hand, confessed all to the Cardinal with theatrical piety, and was rewarded with rapid elevation. New converts cluster around him as a figure of holy cunning. They believe Patmos guided his blackmail as divine providence — literally. This creates a slightly disturbing precedent the older Friars are arguing about nightly.

Fenwick — The Veilwalker Settlement
Fenwick does not react to events the way flesh-born towns do. But the revelation that Gideon Drake — part Veilwalker — attacked one of their settlements centuries ago and cursed souls to hell has reached them. And they have been very, very quiet about it.
The Veilwalkers (Elder Council)
Watchful Grief
Drake's actions — and the Kings' banishment verdict — have reopened a wound centuries old.
Current Sentiment
Trust in Pintland's Rulers30%

The Veilwalkers do not see time as flesh-born do. An attack on a settlement centuries ago is not ancient history — it is recent, by their reckoning. Their settlement was ravaged by Sackbeard. Drake, part of their blood, turned on his own to stop it — and was right to — but then used Veilwalker secrets to curse. That was an abomination.

The Kings exiled Drake to Vodkonia. To the Elders, this means a man carrying Veilwalker secrets is loose in unknown lands. This disturbs them more than anything else in six months.

Spirit-Communers
Grieving
The woodland spirits are restless. The Nether breach has silenced some communion channels.
Current Sentiment
Spiritual Stability22%

When the Veilwalkers transform to commune with woodland spirits, they report a static — a cold silence where certain nymphs once spoke. The Nether breach is blamed. The factory's dimensional tear has made parts of the spirit-realm inaccessible. Among the Veilwalkers, this is equivalent to a human losing sight in one eye.

Trade Vow-Keepers
Steady
Those who trade in vows continue their work. John Cum's legacy of kept promises sustains their faith.
Current Sentiment
Confidence in Trade Vows60%

John Cum kept every vow he made to the Veilwalkers. His death is mourned in Fenwick as genuinely as in Aleforge — perhaps more so, since they understand what his promises meant. The national funeral planned by the Kings is viewed positively. They sent a small gathering of fishing gear as a gift for his passing — which the Kings received without fully understanding the significance.

Young Wanderers
Curious but Unsettled
They want to engage with flesh-born events. The elders counsel patience. Tension follows.
Current Sentiment
Inclination to Act54%

By Veilwalker standards these are young (perhaps only 80-100 years old). They watched Aleforge's upheaval with open curiosity. Some want to travel to the town and commune with the Cloister's mystical foundations directly. The elders say: wait. The earth will call you when the time is right. The young ones are not sure the earth can afford to wait.

Rotto Kaiishi — The Stilted Market Kingdom
Rotto watched the Aleforge chaos from its comfortable ocean perch and saw only opportunity. Factory artifacts, Harrow tourism, and a power vacuum in the region's most important brewing city. Kim Jong Un and the merchant elite are moving quietly but swiftly.
Kim Jong Un & Merchant Elite
Opportunistic
The mayoral vacuum in Aleforge is a chance to install a sympathetic ruler via economic leverage.
Current Sentiment
Strategic Positioning81%

Rotto's elite love disorder in neighbors — it creates dependencies. Aleforge's leaderless state means the Kings are governing by reputation, not mandate. Kim's representatives are already quietly offering campaign financing to multiple potential mayoral candidates, including ones the Kings haven't considered. The Goldcoral embezzlement by Wepple is also known in Rotto circles — it is being held as leverage against Jack Anqoak.

Dock Merchants
Thriving
Factory artifact smuggling through Rotto has created an entirely new underground premium market.
Current Sentiment
Economic Confidence76%

Relics from the Factory — recovered by daring crews — command extraordinary prices when sold through Rotto's underground market. The taboo system pretends not to see it; the dock merchants pretend not to sell it. Everyone profits. The Nether associations actually drive prices up among thrill-seekers and collectors. Six months post-incident, the market is booming.

Common Workers & Stilters
Indifferent
For those who build and repair the platforms, Aleforge's drama is distant news over a bowl of fish.
Current Sentiment
Concern for Events34%

The stilts hold, the market hums, the fish is fresh. The average Rotto dockworker cares little for Pintland politics. That said, the Dread Harrow tourism has brought unusual visitors — scholars, fanatics, adventurers — who sometimes cause trouble on the docks and drive up prices at the food stalls. Low-level frustration is building.

Providence-Rotto Liaisons
Nervous
Providence grows suspicious of Rotto's factory artifact trade. The tonic mineral supply may be at risk.
Current Sentiment
Relationship Stability51%

The Church of Patmos views the artifact trade as spiritual contamination. If Cardinal Addy formally protests, it could threaten the red-earth mineral trade — which both cities depend on. Providence liaisons in Rotto are quietly urging the elite to move the artifact market somewhere even more underground. The elite smile and nod.

The Faiths & Creeds of Pintland
Six months after the Stewards' events, every faith in Pintland has been forced to reckon with the same questions: What lives beyond death? What sleeps beneath the Cloister? And who — or what — truly governs this world?
⚑ The Church of Patmos (Providence)
Core Belief: Divine order through the ClockHeart Tonic, devotion to the unknown gods, and the Cardinal's absolute spiritual authority. The Monk Who Ne'er Slumber'd is their founding prophet.

Post-Stewards View: The Church is quietly electrified. Drake's Veilwalker blood and the Nether portal suggest that the tonic's origins may be older and stranger than doctrine allows. The Califarts are heretically excited. The Cardinal is suppressing their findings. Meanwhile, Cheeks' arrival has given the Church fresh intelligence about Aleforge corruption — viewed as a divine gift.

Overlaps: Respects Veilwalker earth-care as adjacent to Patmos's garden tending. Deeply hostile to Ale-worship and pirate chaos theology. Considers the brewmaster deity of Aleforge a pagan folk spirit, not a true god.
⚑ The Brewmaster Cult (Aleforge)
Core Belief: A deity of revelry and brewing blessed Aleforge's founders with a sacred spring. Annual celebration is religious duty. The Drunken Trials are a holy rite of selection.

Post-Stewards View: The Liquor Kings are now semi-divine figures to the most devout. Buke closing the portal — alone, drinking, thinking — is spoken of as a miracle. Jameson Pilsner's death and resurrection via "magical Aleforge Ale in his blood" has been canonised by street preachers as a genuine miracle of the brewmaster deity. The Cloister's use as a Kings' meeting hall is controversial — some see it as sacred appropriation, others as a blessed union.

Overlaps: Sees Veilwalker gifts as blessings from nature-adjacent deities. Hostile to Providence's demand for sobriety and order. Tolerant of the Tide Callers' ocean mysticism — water and brewing are linked in their theology.
⚑ The Tide Caller Creed (Shanty Town)
Core Belief: The ocean is divine, living, and sentient. It rewards devotion and punishes arrogance. Navigating by its will is the highest spiritual act.

Post-Stewards View: The factory's Nether breach has disturbed their communion with the ocean. The Dread Harrow — a ship of the dead flying above land — is an abomination against sea law. They have formally censured Aleforge in a document delivered to the Windsunk Council: "The landlocked have opened a door the sea cannot close."

Overlaps: Shares spiritual kinship with Veilwalker nature-worship. Distrusts all organised religion, including Patmos — but especially the brewmaster cult, which they view as superstition dressed in a keg.
⚑ Veilwalker Earth-Faith (Fenwick)
Core Belief: The Veilwalkers were born of earth and woodland spirits. They are stewards of nature's balance. Flesh-born are guests on the earth, not its masters.

Post-Stewards View: The revelation of Drake's half-Veilwalker nature has created profound theological distress. A being of earth-blood used earth-magic to damn souls to a dimension of fire. This is viewed as the gravest possible sacrilege within their faith — worse than any flesh-born offense. They have held spirit-mourning rituals for six consecutive moon cycles.

Overlaps: Respects the Tide Callers' ocean devotion as cousin-faith. Disturbed by Patmos's power-hungry hierarchy. Views the brewmaster cult with bemused distance — ale at least comes from grain, grain comes from earth.
⚑ The Ashen Oath (Hidden — Shanty Town)
Core Belief: The world has been irreparably corrupted by grog, piracy, and dark magic. Only total purification — by fire — can restore divine order. They await a sign to act.

Post-Stewards View: The portal, the eternal ghost ship, the resurrected Jameson, the part-Veilwalker diplomat — every event confirms their doctrine. They are reaching critical mass. Unknown outside observers have made contact. The Windsunk Council remains ignorant of their growth.

Overlaps: Paradoxically admires the Church of Patmos's demand for order — but views them as too soft to achieve it. Considers all other faiths symptoms of the disease they seek to cure.
⚑ The Warden Cult (Defunct — Fentmaxxer's Legacy)
Core Belief: Fentmaxxer worshipped the Warden as a supreme weapon-god — a being of sculk and alien biology fallen from space, destined to consume the world and elevate its priest.

Post-Stewards View: Fentmaxxer is dead. The observatory is empty. But his altar beneath the well remains. Six months on, no one has destroyed it. A handful of curious youth in Aleforge visit it. The mystic community is desperately trying to get the Kings to formally seal or destroy the site before it attracts the wrong kind of pilgrim.

Overlaps: Has no true allies. The Church of Patmos views it as the clearest example of heresy they've ever documented. Mystics treat it as a cautionary relic.

The Liquor Kings
JB
Jagerbauhm (Ben)
Liquor King — The Dread Harrow Flyer
Six months on, Ben is the most celebrated of the Kings. His flight aboard the Harrow is legend. He is most likely to win a mayoral election on popular vote. His Providence connections — from the wheat deal — are an asset, though some Aleforge farmers still eye it suspiciously. Currently grappling with Wepple's Goldcoral embezzlement, which implicates his own investment. Torn between justice and loyalty.
SG
Steph "Guinnie" Guinness
Liquor King — The Thatcher Mourner
The statue initiative was Steph's project and his legacy. Six months on, the Thatcher statue stands in the square and commonfolk lay flowers at it weekly. This has made Guinnie the most beloved King by the farmer and brewer classes. His military desertion past is quietly becoming public knowledge — some see it as heroism (he refused to torch a Fenwick village), others see it as a dangerous precedent for a would-be mayor.
BK
Buke
Liquor King — The Thinking Drinker
Buke's moment of strategic clarity — drinking, fighting, and thinking simultaneously to close the portal — has elevated him in the eyes of the mystic and craftsman communities. His docker origins in Rotto give him street credibility that others lack. Currently lobbying quietly for the mayoral seat, he has said nothing publicly. His main weakness: he is beloved, but not eloquent. The political machinery of a mayoral campaign may not suit him.
JP
Jameson Pilsner (JP)
Liquor King — The Resurrected
JP died and was resurrected by the magical ale in his blood. Street preachers have canonised this. JP himself doesn't fully understand what happened. He slept through the First Pour. He nearly died in Aleforge. He is, by common consensus, the funniest and most chaotic of the Kings — which makes him beloved, and impossible to take seriously as a mayor. He is content with this arrangement.
SE
Seamus Bonehardy
Liquor King — Mayor of Aleforge (Elected)
Seamus escaped the sewer confrontation with the eternal pirates, which gave him a reputation for slippery survival — and somehow, improbably, that was enough. He was elected Mayor of Aleforge shortly after the Stewards of Aleforge events, likely because he was the least threatening King to every faction simultaneously: not eloquent enough to be feared, not ambitious enough to be suspicious, and genuinely beloved in a shrug-and-buy-him-a-drink sort of way. Six months into his mayoralty, he has done absolutely nothing. No policies proposed. No legislation passed. No civic initiatives launched. No decrees issued. The town continues to function on the momentum of decisions made during the caretaker period. Merchants are quietly baffled. MAMA finds this almost impossible to satirise. The mystic community is waiting for a sign that will never come. Rump's books remain unaudited. Wepple's situation festers. The Warden altar sits untouched. Seamus, for his part, appears entirely at peace with all of this. He has been seen at the Gilded Tankard most mornings before noon.
JA
Jack Anqoak
Liquor King — Oracle of Aleforge
Jack came from nowhere in particular — a distant land whose name he rarely volunteers — drawn to Pintland by rumour of pirates, mysteries, and the Cloister's pull. He stowed away on a merchant vessel, landed in Rotto, and clawed his way onto a trading-and-piracy ship through sheer labour and stubbornness. He arrived in Aleforge and never left, because whatever force sleeps beneath the Cloister clearly had plans for him. He won the Drunken Trials and has been a King since. In the months following the Stewards' events, Jack received a vision — its origin unknown, whether the Cloister, the brewmaster deity, or something older and stranger altogether. The vision guided him away from Aleforge's streets and deep into the Brandywine Brush, where he found a secluded bar, hidden from the world. He has not spoken at length about what he saw or what the bar means. The title "Oracle of Aleforge" has attached itself to him in the tavern circuits since, spoken with equal parts reverence and bewilderment. His other affairs remain unresolved: Goldcoral Incorporated still bears the wounds of Wepple's gambling, the Gilded Tankard seizure still haunts his reputation among the merchant class, and Rotto holds the whole mess as quiet leverage. Jack's Owe Block freestyle rap has, against all odds, become something of a cult hit across the Pintland Isles — which he seems to find more amusing than anything else.
Town Leaders & Notable Others
SP
Susan Plinket
Moderate Advisor — Secret MAMA Founder
Sentenced to be "Rollo's girlfriend" as a public humiliation. Now operating under pseudonym "Jathan Round" as MAMA's true founder and strategist. Six months on, she has used the punishment to her advantage — playing the role of chastened moderate while secretly coordinating an escalating campaign against the Kings. Her next move is tied to the mayoral election. She is waiting for a King to declare candidacy before striking.
JR
John Rump
Former Royal Treasurer — Embezzler
Cheeks left for Providence and took Rump's secrets with him. Six months of silence. Rump doesn't know if Cheeks spoke to the Cardinal. He doesn't know if the Kings know. He is quietly spiraling, managing his tonic addiction as best he can, continuing to work as treasurer because no one has formally charged him yet. He knows his time may be limited. His books are still cooked. The auditors have not arrived. Yet.
GB
Glub Tuppus Wepple
Brewmaster of the Gilded Tankard
Six months of pretending the Goldcoral investment money wasn't gambled away. Ale sales are good enough that he's slowly been repaying the deficit through organic revenue — but it's taking far longer than hoped. Jack Anqoak doesn't know yet. Wepple is the most cheerful anxious man in Aleforge: all smiles at the bar, all terror in his ledger.
CA
Cardinal Addy
Head of the Church of Patmos
Born to a Shanty whore, risen to divine authority. Six months ago he received Cheeks — and Rump's secrets. He has said nothing to Aleforge. He is letting the pressure build. A man who rose from nothing to command a city by prayer and tonic knows the value of patience. His Califarts' secret research into tonic-Veilwalker connections has not reached him yet. When it does, his world will shift.
GD
Gideon Drake (Banished)
Former Mayor — Now "The Bastard Veil"
Exiled to the unexplored regions of Vodkonia. Six months in, no word has returned from him — but he is part Veilwalker and functionally near-immortal. He carries knowledge of dimensional artifacts, Nether portal mechanics, Veilwalker secrets, and centuries of pirate lore. He is not sitting quietly. He is simply in a place where no one can hear him yet. The Kings made an enemy they may not understand the scale of.
OS
Old Salty
Shanty Town — Barfly, Spy, Scribe
Still writing. Still drinking. Still never sober. Old Salty's pamphlet "On the Matter of the Ghost Ship That Nobody Asked For" has circulated through three ports. The Windsunk Council tolerates him because he always eventually tells them something useful. Currently investigating rumours that the Ashen Oath has received outside correspondence. Hasn't told anyone yet. Probably will. Eventually.

World Tensions — Six Months After
The mayoral question was answered — and then made worse. Seamus Bonehardy was elected and has governed by doing nothing whatsoever. No election drama, no decree, no policy. Just a mayor-shaped void where leadership should be. The longer the vacuum holds, the more bold MAMA becomes, the more Rotto manoeuvres, and the more Providence smiles behind closed doors. The Dread Harrow floats above it all, a permanent omen no one knows how to interpret.

Notable Locations of the Pintland Isles
Beyond the five great settlements lie landmarks that have shaped the history, faith, and fate of Pintland. These are the places that adventurers whisper about, scholars puzzle over, and the Liquor Kings have bled for.
Sackbeard's Tavern
Holy Ground
The shell of the great sea beast — now a tavern and annual pilgrimage site for pirates and traders alike. The most sacred neutral ground in the Pintland Isles.
Origin & History

Centuries ago, a monstrous sea beast awoke and terrorised traders and pirates across the region for months, shutting down all commerce. The great pirate Sackbeard assembled a multinational armada — every notable crew from around the world — and, after weeks of legendary battle, slew the beast. To honour the deed, every crew in the region contributed tribute to drag the beast's hollow shell onto the cliffs and construct a tavern within it.

Purpose & Use

Once a year, every crew in the region lays down arms and comes to the Tavern for a night of drinking, games, and song. This annual gathering — kept secret for years before being discovered — is the oldest neutral institution in the Isles. No blood is spilled here. No debts are called. It is the closest thing Pintland has to a parliament.

Current Status

Six months after Sackbeard's ghostly crew poured through a Nether portal above Aleforge, the Tavern's status is complicated. Some refuse to drink beneath the beast's bones without acknowledging what those bones now represent. Glub Tuppus Wepple manages the Gilded Tankard brewery attached to the structure. Local scholars still debate what caused the original beast to attack — some draw parallels to the other great animal skeletons scattered across Pintland's landscape.

The First Pour Mystery

Legend speaks of a "Pour Eternal" — a cask brewed from the Tavern Beast's blood, stirred with Fenwick spiritweed, and sealed with powdered gold. The maps to its location were scattered in six pieces by the First Captains who feared its power. Whoever assembles all six maps and drinks the Pour Eternal, they say, becomes the next god of pirates. JP notably slept through the First Pour.

The Cloister
Occupied — Kings' Hall
Built by a vanished order of monks atop a hole to Hell itself. Now the Liquor Kings' meeting hall, with an invisible corridor to the aetherial realm of New Haven.
Origin & Architecture

The Cloister was built by an order of monks whose order has since mysteriously disappeared — rumour holds that the Old Prophet murdered them to gain access to the site's power. It sits atop an unexplained natural phenomenon that opens directly to a hellish dimension, which itself covers a prison dimension containing a great beast. Within the hillside are magical dice-lights used by the monks — and now by the Kings — to determine fate.

The Cloister Beast

The beast imprisoned beneath the Cloister is dead, slain by the Liquor Kings during the Cloister Beast Storyline. Edmond Blackmoor preceded them, killed the Old Prophet in a drunken rage, then descended to face the beast himself and died of terror before he could even fight it. The Kings availed where all others failed. A book skeleton of Blackmoor still rests below as a warning.

New Haven Corridor

Upon the Kings' triumphant return after killing the beast, an invisible hallway opened within the Cloister leading to New Haven — an aetherial realm outside normal space, seemingly pre-existing but only now accessible. The doorway is invisible to all except the Liquor Kings. The connection between the Cloister's mystical foundation and New Haven remains unexplained.

Current Status

The Kings use the Cloister as their meeting hall, leaving important decisions to the magical fate-lights. The mystic community now holds meetings here by the Kings' invitation. The Aleforge township has sent annual workers to repair weather damage for decades — the townspeople believe a wizard will curse them if they let it fall into ruin. A beggar (Glub Tuppus Wepple's illegitimate son) was caretaking the grounds and now contemplates his next move with the Kings in residence.

Fentmaxxer's Observatory
Heretical Site
Built in haste to cover heretical beliefs. Fentmaxxer worshipped a Warden-god he sought to raise as a weapon against the world. He is dead. The altar beneath the well is not.
Origin

The astronomer Fentmaxxer built the observatory hastily after observing what he believed was an asteroid striking the hilltop — in truth, the egg of a great beast (the Warden) fallen from space. The observatory was a front for his heretical beliefs. He became a hybrid astrologer-mage, obsessed with the idea of raising the Warden and using it as a weapon against the townspeople and, eventually, the world.

Structure

The observatory consists of a courtyard with a well, a hallway running around the exterior, and a large telescope in a dome-shaped room. Beneath the well in the courtyard lies a demonic altar dedicated to the Warden that Fentmaxxer used to divine information on how to control the beast. A secret room beneath the well was used as a dungeon — Thomas Thatcher was imprisoned here until the Kings found him (and, tragically, left him there).

Thomas Thatcher

Beloved mayor Thomas Thatcher was found caged in Fentmaxxer's dungeon by the Liquor Kings, who continued their quest without freeing him. He died there. Word reached the townspeople. The Liquor Kings' reputation with Aleforge's common folk was severely damaged as a result. Thatcher's death made the subsequent mayoral vacancy that Drake — and then Seamus — filled.

Current Status

Fentmaxxer was killed during the Cloister Beast storyline. The observatory stands uninhabited. The Warden altar beneath the well has not been destroyed. Six months on, curious youth in Aleforge occasionally visit it. The mystic community is loudly and desperately urging whoever holds power to formally seal or destroy the site before it draws the wrong kind of pilgrim. No one with authority has acted.

The Cumstead
In Mourning
John Cum's farm — the Pintland Isles' primary source of wheat and barley. Its owner is dead. Its fields survived a supernatural blight. Its future is uncertain.
John Cum

John Cum was the Isles' primary provider of wheat to local villages, operating an impressive farm with multiple farmhands. Well-off and self-sustaining, his life became hollow after his wife passed away. His mental stability deteriorated visibly in his final months. Strange noises were heard from beneath his house, though no cellar could be found.

The Blight

During the Stewards of Aleforge events, a supernatural blight struck the Cumstead's wheat fields — caused by dimensional artifacts from the Factory that Gideon Drake had buried there to hide them. The blight caused wheat production to collapse, sparking economic panic across Aleforge. An anti-blight ritual using an amethyst shard was performed with 50% listed odds of success — it worked.

John Cum's Death

Driven mad by grief and the blight, John Cum burned his remaining crops and was subsequently murdered — beheaded — with a fake MAMA note staged at the scene. The true killer was Gideon Drake, who murdered Cum to silence him after the farmer discovered Drake burying artifacts in his fields. Drake was later exposed and banished. John Rump stood to inherit the farm, which remains a source of suspicion.

Current Status

A national funeral is being planned by the Kings. The farm loan programme ($400/farm) has been funding new agricultural ventures. But Cum was irreplaceable as a single-supplier anchor for Aleforge's ale production. The Veilwalkers sent fishing gear as a tribute to Cum's passing — they mourn him as one who kept every vow he made to their people. John Rump's inheritance claim is uncontested but regarded with suspicion.

Orchenk Factory
Condemned — Nether Active
Site of the Nethergate disaster. A dimensional portal to Hell was torn open here. It was partially closed. The facility is abandoned, toxic, and full of things that should not exist.
The Disaster

Driven by the desire to master instant travel, the factory's brightest minds constructed the Nethergate Teleporter — a fusion of advanced technology and arcane relics designed to tear gateways through reality. The experiment breached the Nether, a hellish dimension teeming with monstrous beings. Hellish creatures poured through, slaughtered many, and forced survivors to barricade themselves inside. Attempts to deactivate the portal produced a viscous, toxic green sludge that consumed corridors and claimed workers' lives.

The Aftermath

Isolated experiments mutated grotesquely, merging creatures and equipment into nightmarish hybrid forms. Communication ceased. The sludge pools endlessly. The portal remained active, which is what drew Gideon Drake to the Pintland Isles — he feared that the Nether dimension accessed was the same one in which he had imprisoned Sackbeard's cursed crew centuries earlier.

The Portal Closure

During the Stewards of Aleforge events, when Sackbeard's crew poured through the Factory's Nether connection aboard the Dread Harrow, Jagerbauhm (Ben) flew to the ship and placed the Factory's artifacts in a precise configuration that sent the crew back and closed the active portal. The Dread Harrow remained and now floats above Aleforge.

Current Status

The Factory is formally condemned. The portal is technically closed, but the facility itself remains a hellscape of toxic sludge, mutated creatures, and unstable arcane machinery. Artifacts from within have been smuggled out by daring crews and sold through Rotto's underground market at premium prices. The Tide Callers report cold currents and strange fish behaviour near the Factory's coastal position. The portal's dimensional scar has disrupted Veilwalker spirit communion in the area. No authority has any plan to address the facility.

Hoegaarden Hall
Library & Embassy
The grand library of the Pintland Isles. It served as an embassy during the Owe Block events and houses manuscripts of historical and mystical significance.
Function

Hoegaarden Hall functions as the primary repository of knowledge in the Pintland Isles — the closest thing to a neutral scholarly institution the region possesses. During the events of A Walk on Owe Block, Seamus Bonehardy sold recovered Veilwalker relics here for thousands of gold, earning him Providence citizenship. The Kings found safety within its embassy status when Gobbler's authorities pursued them.

Contents of Note

The Hall contains older manuscripts connected to Gideon Drake, which Mr. BBL discovered during the Stewards investigation and which began unravelling Drake's centuries-old connections to Sackbeard. The Old Prophet's writings about the Liquor Kings — recorded while awaiting their arrival at the Cloister — may also be housed here. Reverend Ambrose Ravenscroft began his personal investigation of the amethyst crystals using manuscripts sourced from Hoegaarden.

Scholars of Note

Ignatius De Montfort, a biologist, has been studying the great beast skeletons scattered across the Isles using Hoegaarden resources since the Cloister beast was killed. He has developed fascinating theories he keeps closely guarded after his previous benefactor's profit-hunger clouded the science. Gregor Ashford (the bard of the Gilded Tankard) has also drawn on its records — raising the question of how a frequently drunk bard knows so much of history dating back thousands of years.

Providence Cathedral
Centre of Patmos Faith
The seat of Cardinal Addy's authority. Every three hours, its bell — forged from Rotto's red-earth mineral — calls Providence's Apostles to prayer. The tonic is consecrated here.
Purpose

The Cathedral of Patmos is the spiritual and administrative heart of Providence. Cardinal Addy's voice booms across the city from its spire every three hours, from Tidecall to Bloodwake, summoning Apostles to prayer with the red-earth bell. The ClockHeart Tonic is stored and rationed from its vaults. Tithes — half of all Apostle earnings — flow here and fund a cryptic agenda whispered about even within the faith.

Hierarchy

The Cathedral houses all ranks of the Patmos hierarchy: the Cardinal at the peak, then Califarts (the scholars in towers seeking new tonic formulations), Circs (master brewers working in hidden vats with chants), Friars (roaming market judges), and Sextons (the lowest rank, hauling tithes, prone to petty theft). The recently elevated Cheeks occupies an unusual position — rapidly elevated novitiate with suspicious intelligence-gathering skills that the Cardinal has reframed as divine gifts.

The Night Transformation

When the Hangover bell tolls at night, Providence's people transform — merchants become poets, blacksmiths dance, even stern Friars may weep for joy or rage. This is the tonic's dual nature. Some claim the night's change hides a curse binding souls to Patmos's will. The Cathedral officially calls it a gift of the unknown gods.

Sodomire Gaol
Prison Isle
The prison of the Pintland Isles, located on a rocky offshore position. Its population, management, and current political use are the subject of considerable rumour.
Function

Sodomire Gaol serves as the primary containment facility for the Pintland region. Its offshore position makes escape difficult and administration murky — no single town technically claims authority over it, which means its management falls to whoever has current practical leverage. Historically this has meant Shanty Town maintains informal influence through former pirate captains who ended up inside it.

Current Inhabitants of Note

Several MAA members who survived the Kings' camp raids and subsequent uprisings have been remanded here. The Windsunk Council uses Sodomire as leverage in negotiations — the threat of sending pirates there is a diplomatic tool. There is persistent rumour that one of the anonymous MAMA correspondents operating under the "JR" pseudonym is actually coordinating from within the Gaol, though this remains unconfirmed.

Political Significance

With Aleforge's mayoral seat occupied by Seamus Bonehardy (who has done nothing), and Providence's Church growing more assertive, Sodomire Gaol represents the only hard power the Isles' institutions can deploy. Whoever controls the Gaol effectively has the only credible deterrent in the region. Six months on, this is not lost on Rotto's strategic planners.

Name (The Town)
Frontier Settlement
A small, functional frontier town. Seamus Bonehardy was born here — or rather, arrived here as an infant after his mutineer parents escaped a marooning island. It exists.
History

Name has the distinction of being a town whose name is simply "Name" — suggesting either a placeholder that stuck, a founding joke, or a pragmatic refusal to romanticise. Seamus Bonehardy's parents — both mutineers from different ships who met on a marooning island — made it to Name and scraped together a life here until Seamus was five, when the town was raided by land-pirates and both parents were killed.

Current Status

Name functions as a modest port of call — not grand enough for Rotto's ambitions, not faithful enough for Providence's attention, and not chaotic enough for Shanty Town's taste. It exists in Pintland's blind spot, which is itself a kind of political safety. The Barley Road Initiative under Gideon Drake would have connected it to Aleforge's road network, though with Seamus in office and Drake banished, the project's status is unclear.

Significance

As the birthplace of the sitting Mayor of Aleforge, Name has begun attracting curious visitors — scholars of genealogy, MAMA operatives looking for dirt on Seamus's origins, and Rotto traders smelling opportunity in any narrative that can be bought and sold. The town's residents are largely baffled by the attention.
